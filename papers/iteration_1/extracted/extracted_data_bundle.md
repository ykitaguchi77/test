# Extracted Paper Data

**Paper:** Impact of ophthalmic clinical service use in mitigating myopia onset and progression in preschool children: a retrospective cohort study
**Journal:** BMC Ophthalmology 24, 221 (2024)
**DOI:** 10.1186/s12886-024-03488-5

## Study Design

**Study type:** Retrospective cohort study (Shanghai Child and Adolescent Large-scale Eye Study, SCALE)

**Primary outcome:** Incidence of myopia (defined as SE <= -0.50 diopters) assessed via Cox proportional hazard models

**Secondary outcome:** Annual spherical equivalent (SE) change assessed via generalized additive models (GAM)

**Number of participants:** 5,511 children (from an initial pool of 14,572 children aged 4-6 years, of whom 5,917 required referral)

**Follow-up period:** 6 years (January 2015 to December 2020)

**Key inclusion criteria:**
- Aged 4-6 years at study entry
- Identified as requiring referral to ophthalmic clinical services based on vision screening
- Had two or more vision screening records during the follow-up period
- Had complete personal information (age, sex, district)
- Had available data on ophthalmic clinical service utilization

**Key exclusion criteria:**
- Incomplete records
- Fewer than two vision screenings during the follow-up period

## Results

### Baseline Characteristics

A total of 5,511 preschool children were included in the final analysis. The mean age at study entry was 5.25 +/- 0.82 years, and 2,879 (52.24%) were male. Among these children, 4,184 (75.92%) were classified into the Never group (never sought referral), 1,012 (18.36%) into the Tardily group (sought referral > 3 months after recommendation), and 315 (5.72%) into the Timely group (sought referral within 3 months).

The mean baseline SE was 0.35 +/- 1.02D in the Never group, 0.12 +/- 1.15D in the Tardily group, and 0.08 +/- 1.20D in the Timely group. There were significant differences in baseline SE among the three groups (P < 0.001). The Timely and Tardily groups had lower (more myopic) baseline SE values compared to the Never group.

Regarding district distribution, 1,842 (33.42%) children were from Jing'an, 1,856 (33.68%) from Minhang, and 1,813 (32.90%) from Pudong. The proportion of children seeking clinical services varied by district.

Of the total 5,511 children, 1,327 (24.08%) sought clinical services at an ophthalmic hospital at least once during the follow-up period.

### High-risk Group (non-myopic at baseline)

Among the high-risk group (children with SE > -0.50D at baseline), a total of 1,597 incident cases of myopia were identified over 12,185 person-years of follow-up. The overall incidence rate of myopia was 131.06 per 1,000 person-years.

The incidence of myopia (per 1,000 person-years) differed significantly according to referral compliance: 129.53 for the Never group, 138.34 for the Tardily group, and 118.42 for the Timely group.

The six-year cumulative incidence of myopia differed significantly across groups:
- Never group: 64.76%
- Tardily group: 69.31%
- Timely group: 57.14%
(Log-rank test P < 0.001)

### Cox Proportional Hazard Analysis

In the Cox proportional hazard model adjusted for age, sex, district, and baseline SE, compared with the Never group:

- Tardily group: HR 1.31 (95% CI: 1.10-1.55; P < 0.001)
- Timely group: HR 0.55 (95% CI: 0.33-0.93; P = 0.026)

### Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis

The Kaplan-Meier survival curves demonstrated significantly different survival times to myopia onset among the three groups (log-rank test P < 0.001). The median time to myopia onset was:
- Never group: 4.92 years (95% CI: 4.83-4.92)
- Tardily group: 4.50 years (95% CI: 4.00-4.92)
- Timely group: 5.83 years (95% CI: 4.42-5.92)

### Spherical Equivalent Progression

The annual SE change was analyzed using generalized additive models (GAM). The overall annual SE change was -0.33 +/- 0.37D/year in the High-risk group and -0.08 +/- 0.55D/year in the Myopia group.

Among the High-risk group, the Timely group demonstrated significantly slower SE progression compared to the Never and Tardily groups, particularly at later ages:
- Never group: annual SE change = -0.06 +/- 0.57D/year
- Tardily group: annual SE change = -0.14 +/- 0.50D/year
- Timely group: annual SE change = 0.07 +/- 0.60D/year
(P = 0.005)

### Subgroup Analyses

Subgroup analyses stratified by sex showed similar patterns for both boys and girls, with the Timely group consistently showing lower myopia incidence compared to the Never and Tardily groups. The interaction between sex and referral compliance was not significant (P for interaction = 0.482).

District-specific analyses revealed that the protective effect of timely referral was most pronounced in Jing'an district (HR 0.48; 95% CI: 0.24-0.97) compared to Minhang (HR 0.62; 95% CI: 0.31-1.24) and Pudong (HR 0.58; 95% CI: 0.27-1.25), although the interaction between district and referral compliance was not statistically significant (P for interaction = 0.712).

## Figures

### Figure 1: Kaplan-Meier Survival Curves for Myopia Onset by Referral Compliance Group

Kaplan-Meier survival curves showing the probability of remaining free from myopia over the 6-year follow-up period. Three curves are shown for the Never (blue), Tardily (red), and Timely (green) groups. The Timely group showed the highest survival probability throughout follow-up, while the Tardily group showed the lowest. The curves begin to separate after approximately 2 years of follow-up, with the divergence becoming more pronounced over time. The x-axis shows follow-up time in years (0-6), and the y-axis shows the probability of remaining non-myopic (0-1.0). Number at risk tables are shown below the curves.

**Key data from survival analysis:**
- Median time to myopia onset, Never group: 4.92 years (95% CI: 4.83-4.92)
- Median time to myopia onset, Tardily group: 4.50 years (95% CI: 4.00-4.92)
- Median time to myopia onset, Timely group: 5.83 years (95% CI: 4.42-5.92)
- Log-rank test P < 0.001

### Figure 2: Trajectories of Annual Spherical Equivalent Change by Referral Compliance Group

Line plot showing the trajectories of annual SE change over age (years) for the three referral compliance groups using generalized additive models. The x-axis represents age in years (5-11), and the y-axis represents annual SE change in diopters. The Timely group (green line) showed slower myopic shift compared to the Never group (blue line) and Tardily group (red line). Shaded areas around each line represent 95% confidence intervals. The Tardily group showed the most rapid myopic shift, particularly between ages 7-9. The curves demonstrate that the protective effect of timely referral becomes more apparent with increasing age.

**Key data from SE progression analysis:**
- Never group: annual SE change = -0.06 +/- 0.57D/year
- Tardily group: annual SE change = -0.14 +/- 0.50D/year
- Timely group: annual SE change = 0.07 +/- 0.60D/year
- P = 0.005

### Figure 3: Forest Plot of Subgroup Analyses for Myopia Incidence

Forest plot showing the hazard ratios for myopia incidence comparing Timely vs. Never groups across subgroups (by sex and district). The overall HR was 0.55 (95% CI: 0.33-0.93). Results were consistent across subgroups, with point estimates generally favoring the Timely group. The x-axis represents hazard ratios on a logarithmic scale, with a vertical line at HR=1.0 indicating no difference.

**Key data from subgroup analyses:**
- Overall: HR 0.55 (95% CI: 0.33-0.93)
- Jing'an district: HR 0.48 (95% CI: 0.24-0.97)
- Minhang district: HR 0.62 (95% CI: 0.31-1.24)
- Pudong district: HR 0.58 (95% CI: 0.27-1.25)
- P for interaction (sex): 0.482
- P for interaction (district): 0.712
