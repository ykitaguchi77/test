# Results

## Baseline Characteristics

A total of 5,511 preschool children were included in the final analysis. The mean age at study entry was 5.25 +/- 0.82 years, and 2,879 (52.24%) were male. Among these children, 4,184 (75.92%) were classified into the Never group (never sought referral), 1,012 (18.36%) into the Tardily group (sought referral > 3 months after recommendation), and 315 (5.72%) into the Timely group (sought referral within 3 months).

The mean baseline SE was 0.35 +/- 1.02D in the Never group, 0.12 +/- 1.15D in the Tardily group, and 0.08 +/- 1.20D in the Timely group. There were significant differences in baseline SE among the three groups (P < 0.001). The Timely and Tardily groups had lower (more myopic) baseline SE values compared to the Never group.

Regarding district distribution, 1,842 (33.42%) children were from Jing'an, 1,856 (33.68%) from Minhang, and 1,813 (32.90%) from Pudong. The proportion of children seeking clinical services varied by district.

Of the total 5,511 children, 1,327 (24.08%) sought clinical services at an ophthalmic hospital at least once during the follow-up period.

## High-risk Group (non-myopic at baseline)

Among the high-risk group (children with SE > -0.50D at baseline), a total of 1,597 incident cases of myopia were identified over 12,185 person-years of follow-up. The overall incidence rate of myopia was 131.06 per 1,000 person-years.

The incidence of myopia (per 1,000 person-years) differed significantly according to referral compliance: 129.53 for the Never group, 138.34 for the Tardily group, and 118.42 for the Timely group.

The six-year cumulative incidence of myopia differed significantly across groups:
- Never group: 64.76%
- Tardily group: 69.31%
- Timely group: 57.14%
(Log-rank test P < 0.001)

## Cox Proportional Hazard Analysis

In the Cox proportional hazard model adjusted for age, sex, district, and baseline SE, compared with the Never group:

- Tardily group: HR 1.31 (95% CI: 1.10-1.55; P < 0.001)
- Timely group: HR 0.55 (95% CI: 0.33-0.93; P = 0.026)

## Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis

The Kaplan-Meier survival curves demonstrated significantly different survival times to myopia onset among the three groups (log-rank test P < 0.001). The median time to myopia onset was:
- Never group: 4.92 years (95% CI: 4.83-4.92)
- Tardily group: 4.50 years (95% CI: 4.00-4.92)
- Timely group: 5.83 years (95% CI: 4.42-5.92)

## Spherical Equivalent Progression

The annual SE change was analyzed using generalized additive models (GAM). The overall annual SE change was -0.33 +/- 0.37D/year in the High-risk group and -0.08 +/- 0.55D/year in the Myopia group.

Among the High-risk group, the Timely group demonstrated significantly slower SE progression compared to the Never and Tardily groups, particularly at later ages:
- Never group: annual SE change = -0.06 +/- 0.57D/year
- Tardily group: annual SE change = -0.14 +/- 0.50D/year
- Timely group: annual SE change = 0.07 +/- 0.60D/year
(P = 0.005)

## Subgroup Analyses

Subgroup analyses stratified by sex showed similar patterns for both boys and girls, with the Timely group consistently showing lower myopia incidence compared to the Never and Tardily groups. The interaction between sex and referral compliance was not significant (P for interaction = 0.482).

District-specific analyses revealed that the protective effect of timely referral was most pronounced in Jing'an district (HR 0.48; 95% CI: 0.24-0.97) compared to Minhang (HR 0.62; 95% CI: 0.31-1.24) and Pudong (HR 0.58; 95% CI: 0.27-1.25), although the interaction between district and referral compliance was not statistically significant (P for interaction = 0.712).
