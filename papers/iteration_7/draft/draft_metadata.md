## Writing Decisions

### Title
- Descriptive working title constructed from study content: specifies the intervention (automated micro-sponge eyelid margin debridement), comparator (sham treatment), condition (symptomatic blepharitis), dosing (single session), and design (prospective, randomized, placebo-controlled, double-masked trial).
- No attempt made to identify original paper. Title derived entirely from extracted data.

### Abstract
- Structured format (Purpose, Methods, Results, Conclusions) as required.
- Word count: ~230 words, within the 200-250 target.
- Includes key numerical results with P-values and effect sizes for all four outcomes.
- Conclusion includes a concrete clinical recommendation (against single-session standalone use).

### Introduction
- Three paragraphs only, as required for conciseness.
- Paragraph 1: Background on blepharitis prevalence, pathophysiology, and standard management.
- Paragraph 2: The knowledge gap -- debridement devices lack placebo-controlled evidence.
- Paragraph 3: Study objective stated directly.
- No over-elaboration of general background. Moved directly to the evidence gap and study aim.

### Methods
- All subsections preserved from the extracted study design data.
- Reproduced all inclusion/exclusion criteria, randomization details, intervention descriptions, outcome measures, and statistical methods faithfully.
- Past tense used throughout.
- Sample size section explicitly notes the exploratory nature and absence of formal power calculation.

### Results
- Strictly descriptive with no interpretive language.
- All numerical values, P-values, confidence intervals, and effect sizes reported exactly as provided in extracted data.
- Both tables (baseline characteristics and treatment outcomes) reproduced in full.
- Key finding reported factually: the sham group's significant within-group Efron grade reduction (P = 0.041) stated without interpretation.
- Safety data reported comprehensively.

### Discussion
- Five substantive paragraphs plus Strengths/Limitations and Conclusions subsections.
- Paragraph 1: Summary of principal finding (null result across all outcomes).
- Paragraph 2: Mechanistic explanations for the null result (single session insufficiency, sham partial effect, concurrent lubricant use). Addresses the sham group's significant Efron finding.
- Paragraph 3: Baseline OSDI imbalance and regression to the mean.
- Paragraph 4: Broader context -- procedural placebo effects in blepharitis research.
- Clinical Implications paragraph: Concrete recommendation that single-session debridement cannot be recommended as standalone treatment; advises prioritizing guideline-supported therapies.
- Each paragraph makes one clear point as required.

### Clinical Actionability
- Concrete clinical recommendation included in both the Abstract conclusions and the dedicated Clinical Implications paragraph: single-session debridement should not be recommended as standalone treatment; established lid hygiene and pharmacological therapy should remain the foundation of care.
- Does not end with only "further research is needed" -- research recommendation is secondary to the actionable clinical guidance.

### Handling of Null/Negative Results
- The study produced null results across all primary and secondary endpoints. These were reported factually in Results and interpreted thoroughly in Discussion.
- The paradoxical finding (sham group improved on Efron grading but active group did not) was discussed in terms of placebo effect, partial treatment effect from gel, and regression to the mean.
- The baseline OSDI imbalance was addressed as a potential confound.
- Clinical implications of the absence of benefit were explicitly stated.

### Hedging and Language
- Hedging used economically: "may be insufficient," "suggests that," "may have conferred" -- one hedge per claim, no stacking.
- No filler phrases ("It is worth noting," "Interestingly," etc.).
- Past tense for Methods/Results; present tense for established facts in Introduction and Discussion.
- Third person throughout; "the present study" used for self-reference.

### Effect Size Reporting
- Cohen's d reported for normally distributed OSDI (d = 0.19, negligible/small).
- Rank-biserial correlation (rbc) reported for non-normally distributed outcomes (Efron: -0.095; TBUT: -0.205; Schirmer: -0.053).
- All classified as negligible to small per the thresholds specified in the extracted data.

### Information Not Available / Gaps
- Device brand name not specified in extracted data; referred to generically as "automated micro-sponge debridement device."
- Specific inter-rater reliability for Efron grading not provided in extracted data.
- No information on patient-reported treatment group guessing (validation of blinding success) was available.
- References marked as [REF] placeholders throughout; no literature was accessed.
