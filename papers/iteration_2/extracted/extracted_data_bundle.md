# Extracted Data Bundle: TVC Study 11-Year Follow-Up
## Source: Verma-Fuehring et al., BMC Ophthalmology 25, 340 (2025)

---

## 1. STUDY DESIGN (Minimal)

- **Design**: Prospective follow-up of a single-center, prospective, randomized clinical trial
- **Setting**: University Hospital Wuerzburg, Germany
- **Registration**: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT01228799
- **Original trial**: 62 patients randomized (TE n=32, CP n=30), 2-year follow-up published Matlach et al. 2015
- **11-year cohort**: Of the 2-year completers (TE n=31, CP n=23), 49 contacted, 28 included (TE n=15, CP n=13)
- **Exclusion reasons**: confirmed death (case files), refusal to participate, unknown address, confirmation of death by relatives
- **Population**: Open-angle glaucoma (POAG, PG, PEXG); all Caucasian

### Surgical Techniques
- **Trabeculectomy**: Performed with mitomycin C (MMC)
- **Canaloplasty**: Ab externo procedure with tensioning suture in Schlemm's canal
- **Post-op management**: Antiglaucomatous drugs added for elevated IOP after failed laser goniopuncture (CP) or scleral flap revision/bleb needling (TE)

### Examination Protocol
- **IOP**: Goldmann applanation tonometry, 2 measurements with 1-hour interval (one in miosis, one after tropicamide instillation). No washout of glaucoma medication.
- **OCT**: Heidelberg Spectralis SD-OCT. RNFL G-value used. Q value >20 required.
- **Visual field**: Octopus 900 static perimeter, 30-2 program (Haag-Streit, Switzerland)
- **External data**: Standardized forms for community ophthalmologists (IOP, VA, OCT, VF, surgical history)

### Outcome Definitions
- **Complete success**: IOP at target WITHOUT glaucoma medication
- **Qualified success**: IOP at target WITH or WITHOUT glaucoma medication
- **Definition 1**: IOP <= 18 mmHg
- **Definition 2**: IOP <= 21 mmHg AND >= 20% IOP reduction from baseline
- **Additional success criteria**: IOP >= 5 mmHg, no vision loss, no further glaucoma surgery
- **Hypotony maculopathy**: IOP <= 5 mmHg with macular folds on OCT

### Statistical Methods
- Kaplan-Meier survival analysis with log-rank test
- Mann-Whitney U test (cross-sectional comparisons)
- Wilcoxon signed-rank test (longitudinal within-group comparisons)
- Fisher's Exact test (dichotomous variables)
- Significance threshold: p <= 0.05

## 2. RESULTS

### 2.1 Patient Flow and Follow-Up
- Original randomized cohort: TE n=32, CP n=30 (total 62)
- 2-year completers: TE n=31, CP n=23
- Contacted for 11-year follow-up: 49
- Final 11-year cohort: TE n=15, CP n=13 (total 28)
- Mean follow-up: TE 11.9 +/- 1.1 years, CP 11.0 +/- 2.0 years

### 2.2 Primary Outcomes: Success Rates

| Outcome | Definition | TE (n=15) | CP (n=13) | p-value |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Complete success | Def 1 (IOP<=18) | 53.3% (8/15) | 15.4% (2/13) | 0.06 |
| Complete success | Def 2 (IOP<=21+20%red) | 46.7% (7/15) | 15.4% (2/13) | 0.22 |
| Qualified success | Def 1 (IOP<=18) | 73.3% (11/15) | 69.2% (9/13) | 1.0 |
| Qualified success | Def 2 (IOP<=21+20%red) | 66.7% (10/15) | 76.9% (10/13) | 0.69 |

Overall success: TE 73.3% vs CP 76.9%

Kaplan-Meier survival analysis:
- Definition 1: significant difference (log-rank p=0.03)
- Definition 2: no significant difference (log-rank p=0.09)

## 6. STUDY LIMITATIONS (as stated)
1. Small sample size (n=28) limits generalizability
2. ~50% attrition from original cohort
3. Patient relocation, declining health, mortality challenges
4. Reliance on community-based practitioner data may introduce bias
5. Comparison lacked statistical power (especially for TE)
6. No washout of glaucoma medications performed
