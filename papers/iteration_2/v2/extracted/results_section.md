# Results Section -- Extracted Data

## Patient Cohort and Baseline Characteristics
- Final cohort: 15 TE patients, 13 CP patients (28 total from original 62)
- Mean follow-up: 11.9 +/- 1.1 years (TE), 11.0 +/- 2.0 years (CP)
- Follow-up data obtained via department visits (TE n=10, CP n=11) or from local ophthalmologists (TE n=3, CP n=4)
- Original TVC cohort completing 2-year follow-up: TE n=31, CP n=23
- Exclusions: confirmed death, refusal to participate, unknown address, confirmation of death by relatives
- Of 49 patients contacted, 28 included in analysis

### Baseline Parameters
- Median baseline IOP: TE 20.0 mmHg (IQR 19.0-25.0), CP 22.0 mmHg (IQR 20.0-26.3), p=0.06
- Median baseline medications: TE 3.5 (IQR 3.0-4.0), CP 3.0 (IQR 1.8-4.0), p=0.09
- Baseline BCVA: TE 0.10 logMAR (IQR 0.00-0.28), CP 0.22 logMAR (IQR 0.08-0.40), p=0.13
- Baseline VF mean defect: TE 10.43 dB, CP 4.57 dB
- Pseudophakic at time of surgery: TE 6 (18.8%), CP 5 (16.7%) [based on original cohort sizes]
- All Caucasian population; glaucoma types: POAG, PG, PEXG

## Primary Outcomes -- Success Rates

### Complete Success (without medication)
- Definition 1 (IOP <= 18 mmHg): TE 53.3% (8/15) vs CP 15.4% (2/13), p=0.06 (Fisher's exact)
- Definition 2 (IOP <= 21 mmHg + >= 20% reduction): TE 46.7% (7/15) vs CP 15.4% (2/13), p=0.22

### Qualified Success (with or without medication)
- Definition 1: TE 73.3% (11/15) vs CP 69.2% (9/13), p=1.0
- Definition 2: TE 66.7% (10/15) vs CP 76.9% (10/13), p=0.69

### Overall Success
- TE 73.3% vs CP 76.9%

## Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis
- Definition 1: Survival curves differed significantly at 11 years (p=0.03)
  - Complete success (IOP <= 18 mmHg without medication) significantly higher in TE (53.3%) vs CP (15.4%, P=0.03)
- Definition 2: No significant difference (p=0.09)
- Log-rank test used for comparisons

## IOP Outcomes
- Median IOP at follow-up: TE 10.0 mmHg (IQR 6.0-12.0), CP 14.0 mmHg (IQR 11.5-17.75), p<0.01 (Mann-Whitney U)
- Mean IOP reduction from baseline: TE 11.6 +/- 10.1 mmHg, CP 9.4 +/- 5.2 mmHg

## Medication Use
- Mean number of compounds at follow-up: TE 1.0 +/- 1.4, CP 1.9 +/- 1.5, p=0.17
- Absolute reduction in compounds from baseline: TE 2.13 +/- 1.6, CP 0.31 +/- 1.7
- Significance of medication reduction: TE p<0.01 (significant), CP p=0.47 (not significant) [Wilcoxon signed-rank test]
- Need for >= 1 medication: TE 33% (5/15), CP 77% (10/13), p=0.03 (Fisher's exact)

## Visual Outcomes
- Mean BCVA at follow-up: TE 0.34 logMAR, CP 0.43 logMAR
- Change from baseline: TE 0.1 -> 0.34 logMAR, CP 0.22 -> 0.43 logMAR (p=0.52 between groups)
- VF mean defect at follow-up: TE 14.28 dB, CP 7.29 dB (p<0.05)
- VF mean defect at baseline: TE 10.43 dB, CP 4.57 dB

## Structural Outcomes (RNFL)
- Mean RNFL thickness (G-value): TE 62.4 +/- 19.1 um, CP 66.1 +/- 24.3 um

## Complications
- Hypotony maculopathy (IOP <= 5 mmHg with macular folds on OCT): TE 13.3% (2/15), CP 0% (0/13)
- No bleb-related complications observed
- Original 2-year early postop complications (TE): transient hypotony 37.5%, hypotony-related choroidal detachment 12.5%, elevated IOP 25.0%
- Original 2-year early postop complications (CP): elevated IOP >25 mmHg 30%, hyphema >=1mm 23.3%, hypotony (IOP <5) 20%

### Literature context for complications:
- Transient hyphema common in CP (21-40%)
- Hypotony rates low in CP (0.6-20%)
- TE higher complication rates (7-40%), hypotony maculopathy (4-7%), bleb-related complications (1.6-23%)

## Revision Surgeries
- TE: 20.0% (3/15) underwent subsequent glaucoma surgery [also reported as 26.7% in some sources]
- CP: 23.1% (3/13) underwent subsequent glaucoma surgery
- Time to revision: TE 63 +/- 33.5 months, CP 96 +/- 43.6 months

## Sample Size Calculation
- The Department of Statistics at the University of Wurzburg calculated the sample size
- Required 50% of the original TVC 2015 cohort for follow-up
- Ensured 80% power at a 0.05 significance level
