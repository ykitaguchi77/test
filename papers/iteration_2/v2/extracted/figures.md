# Figures Extracted from Verma-Fuehring et al. (2025)

## Figure 1: Patient Flow Diagram
- **Type:** Flowchart (CONSORT-style)
- **Description:** Shows enrollment from original TVC cohort through to 11-year follow-up
- **Key data:**
  - Original TVC cohort completing 2-year follow-up: TE n=31, CP n=23
  - Exclusions: confirmed death, refusal to participate, unknown address, confirmation of death by relatives
  - Of 49 patients contacted, 28 included in analysis
  - Final cohort: TE n=15, CP n=13
  - Follow-up via department visits: TE n=10, CP n=11
  - Follow-up via local ophthalmologists: TE n=3, CP n=4

## Figure 2: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis
- **Type:** Kaplan-Meier survival curves
- **Description:** Complete and qualified success over time for both treatment groups

### Panel A: Complete Success -- Definition 1 (IOP <= 18 mmHg without medication)
- Shows significant difference between TE and CP (p=0.03, log-rank test)
- TE maintains higher success probability throughout follow-up
- Separation of curves evident over the 11-year period
- TE complete success: 53.3% at 11 years
- CP complete success: 15.4% at 11 years

### Panel B: Complete Success -- Definition 2 (IOP <= 21 mmHg + >= 20% reduction without medication)
- Non-significant difference (p=0.09, log-rank test)
- TE shows trend toward higher success probability
- TE complete success: 46.7% at 11 years
- CP complete success: 15.4% at 11 years

**Note on number-at-risk:** Number-at-risk tables may be included below the Kaplan-Meier curves in the original publication. Specific number-at-risk data at intermediate time points were not extractable from the available sources.

**Censoring criteria:** Patients were censored at the time of failure (IOP exceeding target, vision loss, need for further glaucoma surgery, or IOP < 5 mmHg) or at last follow-up.

## Figure 3: IOP Outcomes
- **Type:** Box plots / IOP comparison
- **Description:** IOP outcomes at follow-up comparing TE and CP
- **Key data:**
  - TE median IOP: 10.0 mmHg (IQR 6.0-12.0)
  - CP median IOP: 14.0 mmHg (IQR 11.5-17.75)
  - p<0.01 (Mann-Whitney U test)
