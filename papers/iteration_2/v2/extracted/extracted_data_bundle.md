## Study Type
Prospective follow-up study of a prospective, single-center, randomized clinical trial (RCT).

## Original Trial (Matlach et al. 2015)
- First head-to-head prospective RCT comparing trabeculectomy and canaloplasty
- 62 patients randomized: TE n=32, CP n=30
- 2-year prospective follow-up at University Hospital Wuerzburg
- Published: Acta Ophthalmologica 2015;93(8):753-761

## Follow-Up Study
- Starting cohort (completed 2-year follow-up): TE n=31, CP n=23
- Exclusions: confirmed death, refusal to participate, unknown address, death confirmed by relatives
- Of 49 patients contacted, 28 included in analysis
- Follow-up via department visits: TE n=10, CP n=11
- Follow-up via local ophthalmologists: TE n=3, CP n=4
- Final 11-year cohort: TE n=15, CP n=13

## Interventions
- Trabeculectomy: performed with mitomycin C (MMC)
- Canaloplasty: ab externo procedure with tensioning suture placed in Schlemm's canal

## Primary Endpoints
- Complete success: IOP within target WITHOUT glaucoma medication
- Qualified success: IOP within target WITH or WITHOUT glaucoma medication
- Definition 1: IOP <= 18 mmHg
- Definition 2: IOP <= 21 mmHg with >= 20% reduction from baseline
- Additional criteria: IOP >= 5 mmHg, no vision loss, no further glaucoma surgery

## Secondary Endpoints
- Changes in IOP, medication use, complications, revision surgeries

## Examination Protocol
- IOP: Goldmann applanation tonometry, two measurements with 1-hour interval (miosis and post-tropicamide)
- No washout of glaucoma medication
- SD-OCT: Heidelberg Spectralis OCT (RNFL G-value; Q value >20 required)
- Visual field: Octopus 900 static perimeter, 30-2 program (Haag-Streit, Switzerland)
- External ophthalmologists: standardized forms provided

## Statistical Methods
- Kaplan-Meier analysis for cumulative success probability
- Log-rank test for survival distribution comparison
- Mann-Whitney U test for cross-sectional analysis
- Wilcoxon signed-rank test for longitudinal comparisons
- Fisher's exact test for dichotomous variables
- Significance: p <= 0.05
- Sample size: 80% power at 0.05 significance, requiring 50% of original cohort

# SECTION 3: RESULTS (All Statistical Findings)

## Patient Cohort
- Final cohort: 15 TE, 13 CP (28 total from original 62)
- Mean follow-up: TE 11.9 +/- 1.1 years, CP 11.0 +/- 2.0 years
- Data sources: department visits (TE n=10, CP n=11) + local ophthalmologists (TE n=3, CP n=4)

## Primary Outcomes -- Success Rates
### Complete Success (without medication)
- Def 1 (IOP <=18): TE 53.3% (8/15) vs CP 15.4% (2/13), p=0.06 (Fisher's exact)
- Def 2 (IOP <=21 + >=20% reduction): TE 46.7% (7/15) vs CP 15.4% (2/13), p=0.22

### Qualified Success (with or without medication)
- Def 1: TE 73.3% (11/15) vs CP 69.2% (9/13), p=1.0
- Def 2: TE 66.7% (10/15) vs CP 76.9% (10/13), p=0.69

### Overall success: TE 73.3% vs CP 76.9%

## Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis
- Def 1: Significant difference at 11 years (p=0.03, log-rank test)
- Def 2: No significant difference (p=0.09)
- Complete success (IOP <=18 without medication) significantly higher in TE (53.3%) vs CP (15.4%, P=0.03)

## IOP Outcomes
- Median IOP at follow-up: TE 10.0 (IQR 6.0-12.0) vs CP 14.0 (IQR 11.5-17.75) mmHg, p<0.01 (Mann-Whitney U)
- Mean IOP reduction from baseline: TE 11.6 +/- 10.1 vs CP 9.4 +/- 5.2 mmHg

## Medication Use
- Mean compounds at follow-up: TE 1.0 +/- 1.4 vs CP 1.9 +/- 1.5, p=0.17
- Reduction from baseline: TE 2.13 +/- 1.6 vs CP 0.31 +/- 1.7
- Within-group significance: TE p<0.01 (Wilcoxon), CP p=0.47
- Need for >=1 medication: TE 33% (5/15) vs CP 77% (10/13), p=0.03 (Fisher's exact)

## Visual Outcomes
- Mean BCVA at follow-up: TE 0.34 vs CP 0.43 logMAR
- BCVA change: TE 0.10->0.34, CP 0.22->0.43 (p=0.52 between groups)
- VF mean defect at follow-up: TE 14.28 vs CP 7.29 dB (p<0.05)
- VF mean defect at baseline: TE 10.43 vs CP 4.57 dB

## Structural Outcomes
- RNFL (G-value): TE 62.4 +/- 19.1 um vs CP 66.1 +/- 24.3 um

## Complications
- Hypotony maculopathy: TE 13.3% (2/15), CP 0% (0/13) -- exclusive to TE
- No bleb-related complications

## Revision Surgeries
- TE: 20.0% (3/15), CP: 23.1% (3/13)
- Time to revision: TE 63 +/- 33.5 months, CP 96 +/- 43.6 months

# SECTION 5: DISCUSSION KEY POINTS

## Main Findings
- After 11 years, TE demonstrated greater IOP reduction and higher complete success vs CP
- Increased risk of complications with TE (hypotony maculopathy exclusively in TE)
- Findings consistent with original 2-year TVC data

## Complete Success
- Medication-free IOP control notably higher in TE
- Clinically meaningful: reduces burden of chronic therapy, improves QoL
- KM significant for Def 1 (p=0.03) but not Def 2 (p=0.09)

## Qualified Success
- Comparable between groups
- When medications permitted as adjunctive therapy, CP achieves similar IOP control to TE

## Safety
- Hypotony maculopathy only long-term complication, exclusive to TE (13.3%)
- No bleb-related complications
- Consistent with known complication profiles

## Clinical Implications
- For patients with moderately low target IOPs who can tolerate some medication, CP remains reliable and safer alternative

## Study Significance
- Longest follow-up of a randomized comparison between TE and CP (to authors' knowledge)

## Limitations
- Small sample size (15 TE, 13 CP)
- Attrition from 62 to 28 patients (~50% follow-up)
- Challenges of long-term data collection (relocation, declining health, mortality)
- Reliance on community-based practitioner data may introduce bias
- Comparison lacked statistical power
- No washout of glaucoma medications
- Baseline VF mean defect difference (TE 10.43 vs CP 4.57 dB) as potential confounder

# SECTION 7: LITERATURE CONTEXT (from verified references)

## Canaloplasty Long-Term Data
- Grieshaber et al. 2010: CP in 60 black African POAG patients, mean baseline IOP 45.0 +/- 12.1 mmHg, complete success 81% (IOP <21) at 36 months
- Lewis et al. 2011: 157 eyes, mean IOP 23.8 -> 15.2 mmHg at 3 years, medications 1.8 -> 0.8
- Bull et al. 2011: 109 eyes, CP alone IOP 23.0 -> 15.1 mmHg at 3 years on 0.9 medications
- Grieshaber et al. 2015: 700+ viscocanalostomy cases, IOP 15.4 at 5 yr, 15.5 at 10 yr, 16.8 at 15 yr; qualified success 40% (European) / 59% (African) at 10 yr

## Trabeculectomy Long-Term Data
- Landers et al. 2012: 234 patients, 20-year follow-up; 57% complete, 88% qualified success; risk factors: younger age, uveitic glaucoma
- Gedde et al. (TVT) 2012: tube vs TE 5-year; TE complications 37% early postop; 29% reoperation rate
- Fontana et al. 2006: TE with MMC, long-term low IOPs in ~50% of cases

## Comparative Studies (CP vs TE)
- Bruggemann et al. 2013: bilateral comparison, IOP reduction similar (CP 50.3% vs TE 53.4%, p=0.87) but CP needed fewer interventions (2 vs 112)
- Klink et al. 2015: QoL higher in CP (57% highly satisfied vs 41% for TE, p=0.034), fewer second surgeries (8% vs 35%)
- Ayyala et al. 2011: 12-month comparison, CP IOP reduction 32% vs TE 43% (p=0.072)
- Meta-analysis (Zhang et al.): TE achieves greater IOP decrease but higher complication rate

## Complication Benchmarks
- Hypotony maculopathy after TE: 1.3-18% (literature)
- Transient hyphema in CP: 21-40%
- Hypotony in CP: 0.6-20%
- TE complication rates: 7-40%
- Bleb-related complications after TE: 1.6-23%
