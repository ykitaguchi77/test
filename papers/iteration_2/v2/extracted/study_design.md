# Study Design Summary

## Study Type
Prospective follow-up study of an original prospective, single-center, randomized clinical trial (RCT).

## Registration
ClinicalTrials.gov Identifier: NCT01228799

## Setting
Department of Ophthalmology, University Hospital Wuerzburg, Wuerzburg, Germany

## Original Trial
- The TVC (Trabeculectomy versus Canaloplasty) study: first head-to-head prospective randomized clinical trial comparing trabeculectomy and canaloplasty
- Published 2015 by Matlach et al. in Acta Ophthalmologica
- 62 patients randomized: TE n=32, CP n=30
- 2-year prospective follow-up

## Follow-Up Study
- Prospective follow-up of the original 2015 TVC cohort
- Starting cohort (completed 2-year follow-up): TE n=31, CP n=23
- Final 11-year cohort: TE n=15, CP n=13 (28 patients total)
- Mean follow-up: 11.9 +/- 1.1 years (TE), 11.0 +/- 2.0 years (CP)

## Participants
- Patients with open-angle glaucoma (POAG, PG, PEXG)
- All Caucasian population
- Follow-up data obtained via department visits (TE n=10, CP n=11) or from local ophthalmologists (TE n=3, CP n=4)

## Exclusion Criteria for Follow-Up
- Confirmed death via case files
- Refusal to participate
- Unknown address
- Confirmation of death by relatives

## Interventions
- Trabeculectomy: performed with mitomycin C (MMC)
- Canaloplasty: ab externo procedure with tensioning suture in Schlemm's canal

## Primary Endpoints
- Complete success: IOP within target WITHOUT glaucoma medication
- Qualified success: IOP within target WITH or WITHOUT glaucoma medication
- Definition 1: IOP <= 18 mmHg
- Definition 2: IOP <= 21 mmHg with >= 20% reduction from baseline
- Additional criteria: IOP >= 5 mmHg, no vision loss, no further glaucoma surgery

## Secondary Endpoints
- Changes in IOP
- Medication use
- Complications
- Revision surgeries

## Examination Protocol
- IOP measured on two separate occasions using Goldmann applanation tonometry with 1-hour interval
- One value in miosis, one after tropicamide instillation
- No washout of glaucoma medication performed
- SD-OCT: Heidelberg Spectralis OCT (RNFL G-value; Q value >20 required)
- Visual field: Octopus 900 static perimeter, 30-2 program
- External ophthalmologists used standardized forms

## Statistical Methods
- Kaplan-Meier analysis: cumulative success probability
- Log-rank test: survival distribution comparison
- Mann-Whitney U test: cross-sectional analysis
- Wilcoxon signed-rank test: longitudinal comparisons
- Fisher's exact test: dichotomous variables
- Significance threshold: p <= 0.05
- Sample size calculation: 80% power at 0.05 significance level, requiring 50% of original cohort

## Ethics
- The study was conducted at the University Hospital Wuerzburg
- Original RCT registered at ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT01228799)
- Ethics approval and informed consent details available in the published full-text article
