## Identified Gaps

### Critical Gaps
1. **Reference list**: Only 1 of likely 20-30+ references could be cited. This is an extraction limitation, not a drafting error.
2. **Missing baseline demographics**: Age, sex, and specific glaucoma subtype counts could not be extracted from the web-available content. The original Table 1 likely contains more complete data.

### Moderate Gaps
3. **Limited international comparison in Discussion**: The draft compares mainly to the original 2-year TVC data and general literature ranges. The original paper likely compares with multiple other studies (e.g., Bruggemann 2013, Grieshaber 2010, Brusini 2014, etc.).
4. **Statistical software not named**: The specific software used for analysis (likely SPSS or R) was not found in extractions and therefore not included.
5. **Ethics/IRB statement**: Not included in the draft, as it was not explicitly extracted. BMC journals require ethics statements.

### Minor Gaps
6. **Some Results description slightly interpretive**: The VF MD sentence in Results contains mild interpretation ("indicating that the between-group difference...").
7. **Revision surgery rate discrepancy**: The source data mentions both 20.0% and 26.7% for TE revision rate. The draft used 20.0% (3/15), which matches the more specific data. The 26.7% may be from a different calculation basis.

## Improvement Rules for Guidelines Update

1. **[Iter2]** For RCT follow-up studies, include an ethics/IRB approval statement and informed consent statement in the Methods, as these are required by most journals.
2. **[Iter2]** When the full reference list is unavailable, structure the Discussion to explicitly note which comparisons come from the original study vs. general literature ranges, and acknowledge the limitation of incomplete citations.
3. **[Iter2]** In Results sections, avoid ALL interpretive language. Replace phrases like "indicating that X was present" with pure data reporting. Move any inferential statements to the Discussion.
4. **[Iter2]** For surgical studies, include a dedicated paragraph in the Discussion comparing revision/reoperation rates with published benchmarks from other surgical series.
5. **[Iter2]** When baseline characteristics show important between-group differences (e.g., VF mean defect 10.43 vs 4.57 dB), explicitly discuss these as potential confounders in the Limitations section.
6. **[Iter2]** For Kaplan-Meier analyses, describe the censoring criteria and include the number at risk at key time points in figure legends when available.
