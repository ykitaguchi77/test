## Study Design

- **Study type:** Prospective, interventional study
- **Participants:** Five subjects aged 29-47 years (mean +/- SD age of 40.0 +/- 7.3 years; 4 males, 1 female), recruited from a university hospital ophthalmology department
- **Inclusion criteria:** Age 20-49 years; ability to avoid excessive UV exposure within 48 h before irradiation and follow-up visits; decimal BCVA of 0.9 or better in both eyes; uncorrected binocular VA of 0.3 or better; no ocular pathologies affecting VA; no use of topical eye or facial skin medications
- **Exclusion criteria:** Contact lens wearer; daily occupational UV exposure; corneal erosion density score >=2 or conjunctival hyperemia score >=2 prior to irradiation; dry eye disease; atopic dermatitis; sunlight sensitivity; participation in other clinical trials
- **Exposure:** 222-nm far-UVC radiation from a mercury-free KrCl excimer lamp unit with a cut-off filter (cutting wavelengths >230 nm, totally cutting >240 nm). The lamp was placed 200 cm from the subject's eyes. Irradiance: 3.1-3.4 microW/cm2. Spectral impurity (235-320 nm / 200-230 nm) was 0.55%.
- **Dose groups:** 22 mJ/cm2 (n=1, 2 h irradiation), 50 mJ/cm2 (n=2, 4 h irradiation), 75 mJ/cm2 (n=2, 6 h irradiation). For 50 and 75 mJ/cm2, a 10-min break was taken every 2 h.
- **Control:** Each subject's left eye served as its own control via custom-made glasses with a UV-cut filter on the left side (transmittance ~0.3% at 222 nm) and no filter on the right side.
- **Stepwise safety design:** 50 mJ/cm2 irradiation was only performed after confirming no significant ocular damage at 1 month following 22 mJ/cm2. 75 mJ/cm2 was only performed after confirming no significant damage at 3 months post-22 mJ/cm2 or 1 month post-50 mJ/cm2.
- **Outcome measures:** Best-corrected visual acuity (BCVA, decimal), spherical equivalent refractive error (SERE), corneal erosion area score (0-3), corneal erosion density score (0-3), conjunctival hyperemia score (0-3, per standard Japanese guidelines for allergic conjunctival disease), corneal endothelial cell density (CECD), presence/absence of pterygium, cataract, and face skin change (erythema, pigmentation changes). Corneal erosion assessed with sodium-fluorescein staining under blue light. Subjective symptoms were also recorded.
- **Follow-up schedule:** Baseline (within 1 week prior to irradiation), 1 day (24 h after start of irradiation), 1 month, 3 months, 6 months. SERE and CECD measurements were omitted at the 1-day examination.
- **Activity during irradiation:** Subjects watched a movie on a TV monitor (196 cm from sofa to monitor) during irradiation. They were monitored via two cameras to ensure they did not doze off or look away.

## Results

During the follow-up period from 1 day to 6 months after irradiation, the safety indices including VA, SERE, corneal erosion area and density scores, conjunctival hyperemia score, and CECD did not change for both eyes of the five subjects. During the same period, pterygium, cataract, and face skin change were not detected for any of the five subjects.

Subject-reported events within 24 h of the start of irradiation were recorded in interviews during Day 1 observations. From the subjects, epiphora sensation, dry eye sensation, epiphora, eye discomfort, conjunctival hyperemia, and mild pain were reported. All reports were for the right eye (irradiated eye), and these events started 2-2.5 h after the start of irradiation and disappeared 4.5-11 h after the start of irradiation. No events were reported for the left eye (control eye).

One subject (UVC04, 75 mJ/cm2) showed a corneal erosion area score of 1 and corneal erosion density score of 1 at Day 1 in the right eye (both were 0 at baseline), while the left eye remained at 0 at both time points. These scores returned to 0 at 1 month and remained at 0 through 6 months.

One subject (UVC05, 75 mJ/cm2) showed corneal erosion area and density scores of 1 in the right eye at Day 1 (both were 0 at baseline), returning to expected levels at later time points.
