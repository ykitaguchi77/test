## Writing Decision Notes

### Study Type and Approach
- **Study type:** Prospective, interventional (dose-escalation pilot safety study)
- **Key challenge:** Very small sample size (n=5) with individual-level rather than group-level data. No statistical tests reported. The paper is inherently descriptive, requiring careful tabulation of every subject's data rather than summary statistics.
- **Writing approach:** Given the absence of statistical inference, the Results section was organized around individual subject data presented in comprehensive tables, with narrative text summarizing patterns across subjects. The Discussion relies on mechanistic reasoning and comparison with general field knowledge rather than effect sizes.

### Title Decision
- Working title: "Ocular Safety of 222-nm Far-Ultraviolet C Irradiation at Doses Up to 75 mJ/cm2: A Prospective Interventional Study With Six-Month Follow-Up"
- Rationale: Descriptive title capturing wavelength, dose range, study design, and follow-up duration. Does not replicate any known published title.

### Assumptions and Inferences
1. **Statistical analysis:** The data bundle provides no formal statistical tests, consistent with the small sample size (n=5). Descriptive reporting was the only appropriate approach.
2. **Ethics statement:** Added IRB approval and informed consent statement per Iter2 guidelines, using placeholder [Institution] since the actual IRB name was not provided.
3. **TLV comparison:** General field knowledge that occupational TLVs for 222-nm far-UVC are approximately 22-23 mJ/cm2 per 8-hour period was used with [REF] placeholder. This is standard domain knowledge in the photobiology field.
4. **Corneal epithelial turnover:** The ~5-7 day cycle time for corneal epithelial renewal is standard ophthalmic knowledge used to explain the resolution of transient erosion.
5. **Dose-response pattern in symptoms:** Described as "observed" in the Results (descriptive) and interpreted in the Discussion, consistent with the Iter1/Iter2 rules separating description from interpretation.
