## Writing Decisions

### Structural Decisions

1. **IMRAD structure followed**: Title, Abstract, Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusions — all present as separate sections.
2. **Abstract**: Structured with Purpose, Methods, Results, Conclusions subsections. Word count ~260 words, within the 250–300 guideline.
3. **Introduction**: 4 paragraphs following background → gap → objective funnel structure. (a) Clinical significance of nAMD and anti-VEGF therapy, (b) concern about IOP spikes and optic nerve effects, (c) RNFL and MRW as measurement tools with literature context, (d) study aim.
4. **Methods**: Organized into subsections: Study Design and Ethics, Participants, Exclusion Criteria, Outcome Measures, Measurement Protocol, Statistical Analysis. Ethics statement and informed consent waiver included per Iter2 rule.
5. **Results**: Ordered as: demographics → RNFL vs. age → MRW vs. age → RNFL vs. injections → MRW vs. injections → age-adjusted analysis. Strictly descriptive — zero interpretive language.
6. **Discussion**: 6 thematic paragraphs exceeding the 5-paragraph minimum: (1) Summary of findings, (2) Differential sensitivity of MRW vs. RNFL, (3) Comparison with prior literature, (4) Mechanisms and clinical implications, (5) Study strengths, (6) Limitations.
7. **Standalone Conclusions section**: Separated from Discussion body per Iter1 rule.
8. **Figure descriptions**: All 9 figures described with content summaries and statistical results per extracted data.

### Content Decisions

1. **Tables constructed from text data**: The original paper had no formal numbered tables. I constructed 5 tables from the text-reported data to enhance readability and facilitate verification.
2. **All numerical values cross-checked**: Every p-value, sample size, mean value, and sector-level result was verified against the extracted data bundle before inclusion.
3. **No data fabricated**: Where specific values were not provided in the extracted data (e.g., regression coefficients, confidence intervals, individual sector p-values for RNFL vs. injections beyond the global value), I reported only what was available and did not fabricate additional statistics.
4. **[REF] placeholders used throughout**: No references fabricated. General medical knowledge used for Introduction and Discussion context (e.g., IOP spikes post-injection, MRW as newer metric) without attributing to specific fabricated sources.
5. **Exclusion criteria faithfully reported**: All 5 exclusion criteria from extracted data included.
6. **Limitation about axial length**: Specifically noted per the extracted data bundle's mention of this as a study limitation.

### Style Decisions

1. **Tense**: Past tense for Methods and Results; present tense for Discussion generalizations and established scientific facts.
2. **Hedging language**: Used "may," "suggests," "could plausibly" in Discussion as appropriate for cross-sectional data that cannot establish causation.
3. **No first person**: Passive voice and "the present study" used throughout.
4. **Abbreviations**: All defined at first use (nAMD, CNV, anti-VEGF, RNFL, MRW, OCT, IOP, PRN).
5. **Exact p-values reported**: All exact p-values from the extracted data reported (e.g., p = 0.005, not p < 0.01).
6. **No emojis**: Per guidelines.

### Blind Writing Compliance

1. **No identifiers used**: No paper title, author names, journal, DOI, or institutional affiliations included — all replaced with placeholders.
2. **Working title is descriptive**: Based solely on the study content and design.
3. **No WebSearch or WebFetch tools used**: All literature context from general ophthalmology/glaucoma knowledge.
4. **Introduction and Discussion written in original structure**: Not copied or modeled from any known published paper.
5. **Author placeholders**: "[Author et al.]" and "[Institution]" used.

### Potential Weaknesses of This Draft

1. **No regression coefficients or confidence intervals available**: The extracted data provided only p-values, not effect sizes or CIs. This limits the quantitative depth of the Results section.
2. **No formal reference list**: All citations are [REF] placeholders. The Discussion would benefit from specific quantitative comparisons with published studies.
3. **Limited demographic data**: Only mean age and mean injection count were available. Sex distribution, visual acuity, specific anti-VEGF agents, and treatment duration were not in the extracted data.
4. **Sector-specific RNFL vs. injections data incomplete**: The extracted data stated "All other sectors: Not significant (individual values not reported)" — so sector-level p-values for this analysis could not be reported.
5. **No information on specific anti-VEGF agents**: The type of anti-VEGF agent (bevacizumab, ranibizumab, aflibercept) was not specified in the extracted data.
