# Reference Mapping: Claims to Supporting References

This document maps each claim in the draft paper to its supporting reference(s), including the location in the paper where citation markers were inserted.

## Introduction

### Claim 1: AMD is among the leading causes of irreversible vision loss in elderly populations worldwide
- **Location:** Introduction, paragraph 1, sentence 1
- **Citation markers:** [1,2]
- **References:**
  - [1] Wong WL et al. (2014) — Global prevalence meta-analysis estimating 196 million affected by 2020, 288 million by 2040. Lancet Glob Health. PMID: 25104651.
  - [2] Thomas CJ et al. (2022) — Comprehensive epidemiology review confirming AMD as leading cause of irreversible blindness in adults over 50 in the developed world. Cureus. PMID: 36337832.

### Claim 2: nAMD is managed primarily through intravitreal anti-VEGF injections
- **Location:** Introduction, paragraph 1, sentence 2
- **Citation markers:** [3,4]
- **References:**
  - [3] Rosenfeld PJ et al. (2006) — MARINA trial establishing ranibizumab efficacy for nAMD. N Engl J Med. PMID: 17021318.
  - [4] Brown DM et al. (2006) — ANCHOR trial demonstrating ranibizumab superiority over verteporfin PDT. N Engl J Med. PMID: 17021319.

### Claim 3: Anti-VEGF therapies revolutionized nAMD management with significant visual acuity improvements
- **Location:** Introduction, paragraph 1, sentence 3
- **Citation markers:** [5,6,7]
- **References:**
  - [5] Martin DF et al. (2011) — CATT trial comparing ranibizumab and bevacizumab, establishing treatment equivalence. N Engl J Med. PMID: 21526923.
  - [6] Schmidt-Erfurth U et al. (2014) — EURETINA guidelines for management of nAMD. Br J Ophthalmol. PMID: 25136079.
  - [7] Ciulla TA et al. (2020) — Real-world visual acuity outcomes in 49,485 eyes with anti-VEGF treatment. Ophthalmol Retina. PMID: 31753810.

### Claim 4: Transient IOP elevation following intravitreal injections, could exert cumulative damage on optic nerve head
- **Location:** Introduction, paragraph 2, sentence 1
- **Citation markers:** [8,9]
- **References:**
  - [8] Hoguet A et al. (2019) — AAO report on anti-VEGF effects on IOP and glaucoma, documenting IOP spikes of 28-55 mmHg at 1 minute post-injection. Ophthalmology. PMID: 30472176.
  - [9] Falkenstein IA et al. (2007) — Documenting IOP changes after intravitreal bevacizumab injection. Retina. PMID: 18040242.

### Claim 5: Debate about whether repeated transient IOP elevation contributes to sustained structural changes
- **Location:** Introduction, paragraph 2, sentence 3
- **Citation markers:** [10,11]
- **References:**
  - [10] Zhou Y et al. (2016) — Systematic review/meta-analysis of 5 RCTs showing increased risk of sustained IOP elevation (RR=3.00). Sci Rep. PMID: 28000707.
  - [11] Mathalone N et al. (2012) — Sustained IOP elevation after bevacizumab injections in nAMD eyes. Graefes Arch. PMID: 22434210.

### Claim 6: Mixed findings in studies on anti-VEGF and glaucoma-related parameters
- **Location:** Introduction, paragraph 2, sentence 4
- **Citation markers:** [12,13]
- **References:**
  - [12] de Vries VA et al. (2020) — Systematic review of 74 articles on intravitreal injections, IOP, and RNFL; found statistically significant but clinically uncertain RNFL reduction. Sci Rep. PMID: 32764619.
  - [13] Shin HJ et al. (2016) — Meta-analysis showing no overall association between anti-VEGF and RNFL loss, but significant loss in high-quality controlled trials. IOVS. PMID: 27077733.

### Claim 7: RNFL thickness established as standard metric for detecting glaucomatous damage
- **Location:** Introduction, paragraph 3, sentence 2
- **Citation markers:** [14,15]
- **References:**
  - [14] Budenz DL et al. (2007) — Foundational study establishing normative RNFL thickness data and age-related decline (~2 um/decade). Ophthalmology. PMID: 17210181.
  - [15] Leung CK et al. (2012) — Prospective analysis of age-related RNFL loss with spectral-domain OCT. Ophthalmology. PMID: 22264886.

### Claim 8: MRW as more sensitive and anatomically precise marker of neuroretinal rim integrity
- **Location:** Introduction, paragraph 3, sentence 3
- **Citation markers:** [16,17]
- **References:**
  - [16] Chauhan BC et al. (2013) — Introducing BMO-MRW as anatomically accurate OCT parameter with enhanced glaucoma detection (81% sensitivity at 95% specificity). Ophthalmology. PMID: 23265804.
  - [17] Chauhan BC et al. (2015) — Multicenter normative study of BMO-MRW and RNFL in normal population, showing ~4% decline per decade. Ophthalmology. PMID: 26198806.
