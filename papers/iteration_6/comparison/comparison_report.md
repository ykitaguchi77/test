## B. Completeness: 8.5/10

### Covered Adequately
- All demographic data from Table 1 -- complete and accurate
- All 11 multivariable ORs from Table 2 -- complete and accurate
- Figure description -- present and accurate
- Female-only subanalysis -- correctly described
- All significant associations -- discussed in detail
- All non-significant associations -- discussed in detail
- Matching methodology -- correct
- Statistical methods (R, epitools, stats packages) -- correct

### Missing or Incomplete Elements
1. **Translational Relevance statement**: The original abstract includes a "Translational Relevance" section not present in the draft. This is a journal-specific section for TVST but is part of the abstract structure.
2. **Univariable analysis results**: The original mentions "Results from the univariable analysis are included in Table A1 which is available in Appendix A" and references ungrouped analyses in Appendix B. The draft mentions the unavailability of univariable ORs in the Limitations but does not reference the appendices.
3. **Specific medications**: The original notes "The most common estrogen-containing medication reported in our cohorts was oral contraceptive pills." The draft does not include this detail.
4. **Specific inflammatory markers**: The original discusses IL-1, IL-6, IFN-gamma, TNF-alpha, and MMP in tear studies. The draft generalizes to "inflammatory mediator release" and "matrix metalloproteinases" but omits the specific cytokine enumeration.
5. **Database name**: The original explicitly names "All of Us Research Program" and "National Institute of Health-funded data initiative." The draft appropriately anonymizes this as "a large national health research database" since this is a blind writing exercise -- this is NOT a gap but a correct anonymization.
6. **SNOMED-CT coding system**: The original specifies cases were identified by "Systemized Nomenclature of Medicine, Clinical Terms (SNOMED-CT)" in addition to ICD codes. The draft mentions only "standardized medical terminology and ICD codes."
7. **Acknowledgments and funding**: Missing from draft, though these are typically not part of blind writing.

### Assessment
The draft captures the vast majority of content. The missing elements are primarily fine-grained details (specific cytokine names, oral contraceptive specification, SNOMED-CT) and supplementary appendix references. The core findings, methodology, and interpretation are complete.
