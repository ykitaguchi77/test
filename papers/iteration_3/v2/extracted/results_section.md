# Results Section: DR and AMD Association (Lin et al., Biomedicines 2024)

## 3.1 Baseline Characteristics (Table 1)

The baseline demographic information and comorbidity conditions of patients in the DR group (N = 3,413) and the non-DR group (N = 13,652) are presented in Table 1.

- **Mean age:** DR group 67.1 years vs. non-DR group 66.5 years (P < 0.001)
- **Age group distribution:** No significant differences (P = 0.999) -- matched variable
- **Sex distribution:** No significant differences (P = 0.999) -- matched variable
- **Comorbidities (all P < 0.001):**
  - Hypertension: significantly higher in DR group
  - Hyperlipidemia: significantly higher in DR group
  - CAD: significantly higher in DR group
  - Stroke: significantly higher in DR group
  - COPD: significantly higher in DR group
  - Liver cirrhosis/chronic hepatitis: significantly higher in DR group
  - Low income: significantly higher in DR group

## 3.2 Incidence and Hazard Ratios of All Types of AMD (Table 2)

**Overall AMD (ICD-9-CM codes 362.50, 362.51, 362.52 combined):**
- DR group incidence rate: **16.77 per 10,000 person-years**
- Non-DR group incidence rate: **5.36 per 10,000 person-years**
- **aHR = 3.50 (95% CI: 3.10-3.95)**

**Stratified findings:**
- AMD incidence increased with age in both groups; association stronger in DR group across ALL age categories
- Highest risk observed in patients aged >85 years
- Both genders had higher AMD rates in DR group; males showed slightly higher risk than females
- DR group had significantly higher HR and aHR regardless of presence or absence of ANY comorbid condition tested (hypertension, CAD, hyperlipidemia, stroke, COPD, cirrhosis/chronic hepatitis)

## 3.3 Incidence and Hazard Ratios of Senile (Unspecified) AMD (Table 3)

**Senile (unspecified) AMD (ICD-9-CM code 362.50):**
- DR group incidence rate: **14.65 per 10,000 person-years**
- Non-DR group incidence rate: **4.78 per 10,000 person-years**
- **aHR = 3.45 (95% CI: 3.04-3.92)**

**Stratified findings:**
- Incidence rate markedly higher with aging in DR group, particularly in 65-75 and >=85 age groups
- DR was a significant predictor of senile AMD independent of demographic factors and comorbid conditions

## 3.4 Incidence and Hazard Ratios of Non-Exudative AMD (Table 4)

**Non-exudative AMD (ICD-9-CM code 362.51):**
- **aHR = 2.92 (95% CI: 2.08-4.09)**
- Male DR patients had higher incidence rate of non-exudative AMD compared to female DR patients
- Specific incidence rates per 10,000 PYs not extracted for this subtype

## 3.5 Incidence and Hazard Ratios of Exudative AMD (Table 5)

**Exudative AMD (ICD-9-CM code 362.52):**
- DR group incidence rate: **1.37 per 10,000 person-years**
- Non-DR group incidence rate: **0.31 per 10,000 person-years**
- **aHR = 3.92 (95% CI: 2.51-6.14)**

**Stratified findings:**
- Notably higher incidence rate of exudative AMD in DR group
- aHR remained significantly elevated after adjusting for confounders
- Risks influenced by cardiovascular comorbid conditions (hypertension, hyperlipidemia, CAD)

## 3.6 Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis

- Significantly higher cumulative incidence of AMD in DR group vs. non-DR group
- **Log-rank test: P < 0.001**
- Cumulative incidence curves diverged progressively over the follow-up period
- All AMD subtypes showed significantly higher cumulative incidence in DR group (Figures 2-5)

## Note on Unadjusted Hazard Ratios

The original Tables 2-5 contain columns for both unadjusted HR (with 95% CI) and adjusted HR (aHR, with 95% CI). Only the aHR values were extractable from the available sources. The presence of both HR and aHR in the original tables allows readers to assess the impact of covariate adjustment. This limitation is explicitly noted per guideline [Iter3, Technical Rule 13].
