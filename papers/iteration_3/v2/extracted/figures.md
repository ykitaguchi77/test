# Figure Descriptions: DR and AMD Association (Lin et al., Biomedicines 2024)

## Figure 1: Study Flow Chart
- **Type:** Patient selection flow diagram
- **Caption:** Flow chart of patient selection
- **Description:** Patients were identified from the Longitudinal Health Insurance Database (LHID) 2000, which contains data for one million randomly sampled patients from the Taiwan National Health Insurance Database. Patients aged >=55 years with newly diagnosed diabetic retinopathy (ICD-9-CM codes 362.01, 362.02) between 2000 and 2006 were selected as the study cohort. Patients with prior DR or DM (ICD-9-CM 250) diagnoses before 2000 were excluded. The non-DR group excluded individuals with a history of AMD prior to the index date. For each DR patient, four non-DR controls matched by age and sex were selected.
- **Key numbers:** DR group N = 3,413; Non-DR group N = 13,652; Matching ratio 1:4
- **Source database:** LHID 2000 (1 million patients)

## Figure 2: Kaplan-Meier Cumulative Incidence -- All Types of AMD
- **Type:** Kaplan-Meier survival curve
- **Caption:** Kaplan-Meier curves showing the cumulative risk of all types of macular degeneration (ICD-9-CM codes 362.50, 362.51, 362.52) in the DR group compared to the non-DR group
- **Key finding:** DR group demonstrated significantly higher cumulative incidence of overall AMD (log-rank test, P < 0.001)
- **Curves:** Two lines -- DR group (higher cumulative incidence) vs. non-DR group (lower cumulative incidence)
- **Time axis:** Follow-up period (years from index date)
- **Number-at-risk data:** Not available from extraction

## Notes on Figures
- All Kaplan-Meier curves showed progressive divergence of cumulative incidence between DR and non-DR groups over the follow-up period.
- The log-rank test result (P < 0.001) is explicitly reported for overall AMD (Figure 2).
- Number-at-risk tables were not available from web-based extraction; it is unclear whether the original figures include them.
- Actual image files could not be reproduced; descriptions are based on captions and text references in the paper.
