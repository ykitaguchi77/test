## Table 2. Incidence and Estimated Hazard Ratios of All Types of AMD (ICD-9-CM Codes 362.50, 362.51, 362.52)

### Overall

| Group | N | Incidence Rate (per 10,000 PYs) | aHR (95% CI) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Non-DR | 13,652 | 5.36 | 1.00 (Reference) |
| DR | 3,413 | 16.77 | 3.50 (3.10-3.95) |

### Stratified Findings (Table 2 structure)

The original Table 2 is stratified by the following strata, each containing: N, events, person-years, incidence rate per 10,000 PYs, HR (95% CI), and aHR (95% CI):

**Age subgroups:**
- 55-64: Higher incidence in DR group vs non-DR group
- 65-74: Higher incidence in DR group vs non-DR group
- 75-84: Higher incidence in DR group vs non-DR group
- >=85: Highest risk observed; strongest DR-AMD association

**Sex:**
- Male: Higher incidence in DR group; slightly higher risk than females
- Female: Higher incidence in DR group

**Comorbidity strata (with/without each):**
- Hypertension: Significantly higher HR and aHR in DR group regardless
- Hyperlipidemia: Significantly higher HR and aHR in DR group regardless
- CAD: Significantly higher HR and aHR in DR group regardless
- Stroke: Significantly higher HR and aHR in DR group regardless
- COPD: Significantly higher HR and aHR in DR group regardless
- Liver cirrhosis/chronic hepatitis: Significantly higher HR and aHR in DR group regardless

**Note:** Exact stratified cell values (events, person-years, HR, aHR for each stratum) were not individually extractable from web sources. The overall incidence rates and aHR are confirmed. The table structure includes both unadjusted HR and adjusted HR (aHR) columns in the original.

Model adjusted for: age, sex, low income, hypertension, hyperlipidemia, CAD, stroke, COPD, and liver cirrhosis/chronic hepatitis.

## Table 3. Incidence and Estimated Hazard Ratios of Senile (Unspecified) AMD (ICD-9-CM Code 362.50)

### Overall

| Group | N | Incidence Rate (per 10,000 PYs) | aHR (95% CI) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Non-DR | 13,652 | 4.78 | 1.00 (Reference) |
| DR | 3,413 | 14.65 | 3.45 (3.04-3.92) |

### Stratified Findings

Same stratification structure as Table 2 (age, sex, comorbidity with/without).

- Incidence rate markedly higher with aging in DR group, particularly in 65-75 and >=85 age groups.
- DR is a significant predictor of senile AMD independent of demographic factors and comorbid conditions.

Model adjusted for: age, sex, low income, hypertension, hyperlipidemia, CAD, stroke, COPD, and liver cirrhosis/chronic hepatitis.

## Table 5. Incidence and Estimated Hazard Ratios of Exudative AMD (ICD-9-CM Code 362.52)

|---|---|---|---|
| Non-DR | 13,652 | 0.31 | 1.00 (Reference) |
| DR | 3,413 | 1.37 | 3.92 (2.51-6.14) |

### Stratified Findings

Same stratification structure as Tables 2-4.

## Summary Table: Adjusted Hazard Ratios Across All AMD Subtypes

| AMD Subtype | ICD-9-CM Code | DR Incidence Rate (per 10,000 PYs) | Non-DR Incidence Rate (per 10,000 PYs) | aHR (95% CI) |

**Key observation:** Exudative AMD had the highest aHR (3.92), while non-exudative AMD had the lowest (2.92). Overall AMD showed the most precise CI (3.10-3.95) due to the largest number of events.
