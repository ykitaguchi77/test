## Top 10 Verified References (Most Important for the Study)

### 1. He MS, Chang FL, Lin HZ, Wu JL, Hsieh TC, Lee YC.
The association between diabetes and age-related macular degeneration among the elderly in Taiwan. *Diabetes Care*. 2018;41(10):2202-2211. doi:10.2337/dc18-0707. PMID: 30061321.
- **Relevance:** Key comparator study. Taiwan LHID. Reported nonexudative AMD HR 3.89 (P=0.001) and exudative AMD HR 3.42 (P<0.001) in diabetes with DR cohorts vs diabetes without DR. 71,904 DM patients, 270,213 non-DM controls aged >=50.

### 2. Hahn P, Acquah K, Cousins SW, Lee PP, Sloan FA.
Ten-year incidence of age-related macular degeneration according to diabetic retinopathy classification among Medicare beneficiaries. *Retina*. 2013;33(5):911-919. doi:10.1097/IAE.0b013e3182831248. PMID: 23407352.
- **Relevance:** US Medicare study. NPDR associated with dry AMD (HR 1.24; 95% CI: 1.08-1.43) and wet AMD (HR 1.68; 95% CI: 1.23-2.31). PDR associated with wet AMD only (HR 2.15; 95% CI: 1.07-4.33).

### 3. Chen X, Rong SS, Xu Q, Tang FY, Liu Y, Gu H, Tam PO, Chen LJ, Brelen ME, Pang CP, Zhao C.
Diabetes mellitus and risk of age-related macular degeneration: a systematic review and meta-analysis. *PLoS One*. 2014;9(9):e108196. doi:10.1371/journal.pone.0108196. PMID: 25238063.
- **Relevance:** Systematic review/meta-analysis. 24 articles, 27 study populations. DM associated with AMD (cohort OR 1.05; 95% CI: 1.00-1.11).

### 4. Wang J, Li M, Geng Z, Khattak S, Ji X, Wu D, Dang Y.
The association between diabetic retinopathy and the prevalence of age-related macular degeneration -- the Kailuan Eye Study. *Frontiers in Public Health*. 2022;10:922289. doi:10.3389/fpubh.2022.922289.
- **Relevance:** Chinese population-based study. 14,440 participants. DR (not DM alone) was significant risk factor for dry AMD (prevalence 20.8% vs 16.0%, P significant). No significant association with wet AMD.

### 5. Wong WL, Su X, Li X, Cheung CM, Klein R, Cheng CY, Wong TY.
Global prevalence of age-related macular degeneration and disease burden projection for 2020 and 2040: a systematic review and meta-analysis. *Lancet Global Health*. 2014;2(2):e106-e116. doi:10.1016/S2214-109X(13)70145-1. PMID: 25104651.
- **Relevance:** Establishes global AMD burden. 196 million affected in 2020, projected 288 million by 2040.

### 6. Yau JW, Rogers SL, Kawasaki R, et al.; META-EYE Study Group.
Global prevalence and major risk factors of diabetic retinopathy. *Diabetes Care*. 2012;35(3):556-564. doi:10.2337/dc11-1909. PMID: 22301125.
- **Relevance:** Establishes global DR prevalence and risk factors. Pooled analysis of 22,896 individuals.

### 7. Teo ZL, Tham YC, Yu M, et al.
Global prevalence of diabetic retinopathy and projection of burden through 2045: systematic review and meta-analysis. *Ophthalmology*. 2021;128(11):1580-1591. doi:10.1016/j.ophtha.2021.04.027. PMID: 33940045.
- **Relevance:** Updated global DR prevalence: 22.27% among diabetics. Projected 103 million in 2020 to 160.5 million by 2045.

### 8. Kauppinen A, Paterno JJ, Blasiak J, Salminen A, Kaarniranta K.
Inflammation and its role in age-related macular degeneration. *Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences*. 2016;73(9):1765-1786. doi:10.1007/s00018-016-2147-8. PMID: 26852158.
- **Relevance:** Comprehensive review of inflammatory mechanisms in AMD. Supports biological plausibility of DR-AMD link.

### 9. Donath MY, Shoelson SE.
Type 2 diabetes as an inflammatory disease. *Nature Reviews Immunology*. 2011;11(2):98-107. doi:10.1038/nri2925. PMID: 21233852.
- **Relevance:** Establishes diabetes as inflammatory disease. Supports shared inflammatory pathways between DR and AMD.

### 10. Adamis AP, Miller JW, Bernal MT, D'Amico DJ, Folkman J, Yeo TK, Yeo KT.
Increased vascular endothelial growth factor levels in the vitreous of eyes with proliferative diabetic retinopathy. *American Journal of Ophthalmology*. 1994;118(4):445-450. doi:10.1016/S0002-9394(14)75794-0. PMID: 7943121.
- **Relevance:** Landmark study on VEGF in proliferative DR. Supports shared VEGF pathway between DR and exudative AMD.
