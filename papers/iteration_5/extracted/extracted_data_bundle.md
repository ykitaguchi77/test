## Study Design

- **Study type**: Prospective, longitudinal, non-interventional, observational cohort study
- **Setting**: Primary care centers across the United States, using a historical database of 7-field color fundus photograph images from a retinal imaging company. De-identified data were purchased by a pharmaceutical company from the retinal imaging company, which was previously used for monitoring DR in the DM primary care setting. Given the study utilizes a database of images, information on management of patients before and after imaging is not available.
- **Participants**: Adults aged ≥ 18 years with diabetes mellitus (DM) who underwent DR screening in either eye between January 1999 and December 2016. Only patients with images associated with ≥ 2 valid ETDRS-DRSS assessments in either eye were included.
- **Total population**: 22,109 patients (41,977 eyes) selected from 73,321 unique patients with DM in the database (51,212 excluded due to no images or no gradable images at ≥ 2 visits)
- **Follow-up period**: Over 5 years (data collection January 1999 – December 2016)
- **Ethics**: Approved by a university health sciences center IRB. Participants provided written informed consent prior to capture of photographs.
- **Imaging limitations**: Due to the historical nature of the database, optical coherence tomography images are not available and patient data are limited to age, sex, race, and diabetes type.

### DR Severity Classification
DR severity was classified according to the Early Treatment Diabetic Retinopathy Study-Diabetic Retinopathy Severity Scale (ETDRS-DRSS):
- Absent (level 10)
- Mild microaneurysm only (level 20)
- Mild NPDR (level 35)
- Moderate NPDR (level 43)
- Moderately severe NPDR (level 47)
- Severe NPDR (level 53)
- Mild PDR (level 61)
- Moderate PDR (level 65)
- High-risk PDR (level 71/75)
- Advanced PDR (level 81/85)

### Image Grading
- 7-field color fundus photographs graded by professional graders at a centralized reading center at a university medical center
- Images were double graded by readers masked to the other readers' results, with independent third reader adjudication

### CSME Definition
CSME defined by ETDRS criteria: retinal thickening and/or hard exudates adjacent to thickening occurring at or within 500 μm of the fovea

### Primary Outcomes
1. ≥ 2-step DR worsening (≥ 2-step increase on ETDRS-DRSS compared with initial assessment)
2. Development of CSME
3. Development of PDR
4. Development of CSME and PDR together

### Subanalyses
- Impact of race on time to first ≥ 2-step DR worsening
- Impact of DM type on time to first ≥ 2-step DR worsening

### Statistical Analysis
- Kaplan-Meier method for time-to-event distributions (time to first ≥ 2-step DR worsening, development of CSME, PDR, or CSME and PDR)
- Time to first ≥ 2-step DR worsening summarized by initial DRSS score

### Number at Risk Definition
Patients who had color fundus photographs evaluable for the DRSS score in ≥ 2 visits in either eye were included. "Number of eyes at risk of developing DR at year N" refers to the number of eyes from patients who had ≥ N years of follow-up data available.

## Results

### Patient Characteristics
Between January 1999 and December 2016, the database collected images and data from 73,321 unique patients with DM. A total of 51,212 patients were excluded due to no images, or no gradable images at ≥ 2 visits; the remaining 22,109 patients (41,997 eyes) constituted the study population (Fig. 1). Baseline patient demographics are presented in Table 1; median age was 61 years and most patients with known status were male (83.0%). Only a subset of patients (n = 4,085) reported their race or ethnicity; most patients who provided this information were White (48.0%) or Native American (41.0%). Only a subset of patients (n = 11,334) reported diabetes type; most patients who provided this information had type 2 diabetes (94.2%).

### Progression of DR by ETDRS-DRSS
For all eyes evaluated, the proportion of eyes with ≥ 2-step DR worsening was 2.7% at year 2, 7.1% at year 4, and 9.6% at year 5 (Fig. 2). As expected, the percentage of eyes with ≥ 2-step DR worsening after year 2 was greatest among eyes with moderate-to-severe NPDR (level 43–53) at baseline (Fig. 3). For the pooled moderate-to-severe NPDR (level 43–53) eyes at baseline, the proportion of eyes with ≥ 2-step worsening was 11.6% at year 2, 26.4% at year 4, and 36.4% at year 5 in this subset (Fig. 4). Similar findings were reported among eyes with moderately severe to severe NPDR (level 47–53) at baseline (Supplementary Fig. 2).

### Progression of DR by PDR and/or CSME
Analysis of PDR and/or CSME development over time in all available eyes with no CSME and no PDR (ETDRS-DRSS 10–53) at baseline suggested the existence of 3 distinct clinical phenotypes: 1 progressed to CSME (1.24% at year 5), another progressed to PDR (0.49% at year 5), and only a small subset experienced progression to both vision-threatening forms of DR (0.10% at year 5; Fig. 5a). Existence of these 3 distinct clinical DR phenotypes did not appear to be driven by baseline DR severity: similar trends of distinct clinical progression subtypes were observed in eyes with no CSME and moderate-to-severe NPDR (ETDRS-DRSS 43–53) at baseline (Fig. 5b), and no CSME and moderately severe to severe NPDR (ETDRS-DRSS 47–53) at baseline (Fig. 5c). Patients with moderate-to-severe NPDR at baseline experienced higher rates of progression to PDR or CSME compared with the overall population with DR.

### Subanalysis by Race
In the subanalysis of all eyes categorized by race, the rate of ≥ 2-step worsening was higher in Black and White patients compared with Native American patients. By year 2, the proportion of eyes with ≥ 2-step worsening was 7.6% in Black, 4.8% in White, and 1.6% in Native American patients (Fig. 6).

### Subanalysis by DM Type
In the subanalysis of all eyes categorized by DM type, the estimated proportion of DR worsening by ≥ 2 steps was higher with type 1 DM compared with type 2 DM (Fig. 7).

### Note on Eye Count
The study design and Table 1 report 41,977 eyes, while Fig. 2 caption and patient characteristics text report 41,997 eyes. Both values are present in the source.
