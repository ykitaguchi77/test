# Study Design Summary

## Study Type
Prospective, longitudinal, non-interventional, observational cohort study

## Setting
Primary care centers across a large country, using a historical database of 7-field color fundus photograph images from a retinal imaging company.

## Participants
- Adults aged ≥ 18 years with diabetes mellitus (DM) who underwent DR screening in either eye between January 1999 and December 2016
- Only patients with images associated with ≥ 2 valid ETDRS-DRSS assessments in either eye were included
- Total: 22,109 patients (41,977 eyes) from 73,321 unique patients in the database (51,212 excluded due to no images or no gradable images at ≥ 2 visits)

## DR Severity Classification
DR severity was classified according to the Early Treatment Diabetic Retinopathy Study-Diabetic Retinopathy Severity Scale (ETDRS-DRSS), categorizing DR into levels:
- Absent (level 10)
- Mild microaneurysm only (level 20)
- Mild NPDR (level 35)
- Moderate NPDR (level 43)
- Moderately severe NPDR (level 47)
- Severe NPDR (level 53)
- Mild PDR (level 61)
- Moderate PDR (level 65)
- High-risk PDR (level 71/75)
- Advanced PDR (level 81/85)

## Image Grading
- 7-field color fundus photographs graded by professional graders at a centralized reading center at a university medical center
- Images were double graded by readers masked to the other readers' results, with independent third reader adjudication

## Primary Outcomes
1. ≥ 2-step DR worsening (≥ 2-step increase on ETDRS-DRSS compared with initial assessment)
2. Development of CSME (ETDRS criteria: retinal thickening and/or hard exudates adjacent to thickening at or within 500 μm of the fovea)
3. Development of PDR
4. Development of CSME and PDR together

## Subanalyses
- Impact of race on time to first ≥ 2-step DR worsening
- Impact of DM type on time to first ≥ 2-step DR worsening

## Statistical Analysis
- Kaplan-Meier method for time-to-event distributions (time to first ≥ 2-step DR worsening, development of CSME, PDR, or CSME and PDR)
- Time to first ≥ 2-step DR worsening summarized by initial DRSS score

## Follow-up Period
Over 5 years (data collection January 1999 – December 2016)

## Ethics
- Approved by a university health sciences center IRB
- Participants provided written informed consent prior to capture of photographs

## Limitations (from study design)
- Historical database; no OCT or OCTA imaging available
- Limited patient clinical data (age, sex, race, diabetes type only)
- No information on patient management before/after imaging
- Patients did not return at regularly scheduled follow-up visits
- Excludes patients who transitioned to specialty care (potential underestimation of progression)
- Incomplete reporting of patient demographics, especially race
