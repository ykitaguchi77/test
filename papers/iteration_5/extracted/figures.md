# Figures

## Figure 1: Patient Selection Flowchart
- **Type**: Flowchart
- **Description**: Patient selection flowchart showing exclusion criteria
- **Key data**:
  - Total database: 73,321 unique patients with DM
  - Excluded: 51,212 (no images or no gradable images at ≥ 2 visits)

## Figure 2: ≥ 2-step DR Worsening in All Eyes
- **Type**: Kaplan-Meier curve with 95% CI shaded region
- **Population**: 41,997 eyes from 22,109 patients (all eyes)
- **Key data points from text**:
  - Year 2: 2.7%
  - Year 4: 7.1%
  - Year 5: 9.6%
- **Axes**: X-axis = time (years), Y-axis = proportion with ≥ 2-step DR worsening
- **Note**: Number of eyes at risk shown at each year

## Figure 3: Percentage of Eyes with ≥ 2-step DR Worsening at Year 2 by Baseline DR Severity
- **Type**: Bar chart
- **Description**: Shows percentage of eyes with ≥ 2-step DR worsening at year 2 based on baseline DR severity level
- **Key finding**: Moderate-to-severe NPDR eyes (highlighted with yellow box) had the highest worsening rates
- **Note**: Each eye could be included in multiple baseline severity subtypes

## Figure 5: Development of CSME, PDR, or CSME and PDR Over Time
- **Type**: Kaplan-Meier curves (3 panels: a, b, c), each showing 3 lines (CSME, PDR, CSME+PDR)
- **Panel a**: Eyes with no CSME and ETDRS-DRSS 10–53 at baseline
  - Population: 21,757 patients and 41,272 eyes
  - CSME at year 5: 1.24%
  - PDR at year 5: 0.49%
  - CSME and PDR at year 5: 0.10%
- **Panel b**: Eyes with no CSME and ETDRS-DRSS 43–53 at baseline
  - Population: 810 patients and 1,046 eyes
  - Key finding: Higher rates of progression to PDR or CSME compared with overall population, but same 3 distinct phenotype pattern
- **Panel c**: Eyes with no CSME and ETDRS-DRSS 47–53 at baseline
  - Population: 265 patients and 310 eyes
  - Key finding: Similar distinct clinical progression subtypes observed
- **Key finding**: 3 distinct clinical DR phenotypes exist (CSME-only, PDR-only, both), not dependent on baseline DRSS
- **Note**: 95% CI shaded regions; number of eyes at risk shown at each year

## Figure 6: ≥ 2-step DR Worsening by Race for All Eyes
- **Type**: Kaplan-Meier curves by race with 95% CI shaded regions
- **Key data points from text (at year 2)**:
  - Black patients: 7.6%
  - White patients: 4.8%
  - Native American patients: 1.6%
- **Key finding**: Higher rate of ≥ 2-step worsening in Black and White patients compared with Native American patients

## Figure 7: ≥ 2-step DR Worsening by Diabetes Mellitus Type for All Eyes
- **Type**: Kaplan-Meier curves by DM type with 95% CI shaded regions
- **Key finding**: Higher estimated proportion of DR worsening by ≥ 2 steps with type 1 DM compared with type 2 DM
- **Note**: Specific percentage values not provided in the text
